***

The bright Hawaiian flower featured in the photograph is a hibiscus, a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae. It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the world, including the Hawaiian Islands. The hibiscus is the state flower of Hawaii and is a popular flower used in leis, or flower necklaces.

The hibiscus has a large, showy flower with five petals. The flower can be found in a variety of colors, including red, pink, yellow, orange, and white. The hibiscus is known for its beauty and is often used in landscaping, floral arrangements, and as a decorative element in clothing and jewelry.

Hibiscus flowers are also known for their medicinal properties. The flowers are used to make teas and tinctures that are believed to have health benefits. The flowers are also used in traditional Chinese medicine to treat a variety of ailments, including high blood pressure and digestive problems.
