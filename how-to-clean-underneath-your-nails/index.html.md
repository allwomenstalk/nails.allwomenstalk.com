# How to Clean underneath Your Nails ...

You wash your hair and your body every single day, but how often do you clean your nails? Sure, you'll use remover to take the polish off, but you should be doing a little bit more than that. After all, you don't want your nails to look disgusting on the days you don't have time to paint them. To make sure they always look their best, here are a few tips for cleaning underneath them:

## 1. Use the Tip of a Nail Pick

![Use the Tip of a Nail Pick](https://img.allw.mn/content/ia/dw/r56b8jvn568e7861a5f0e686968035_500x256.gif)

This is the easiest way to clean under your nails. All you have to do is buy a nail pick, which is usually included with your nail clippers. Once your hands are dry, you can use the tip of that pick to dig beneath your nail. Rub a tissue against the pick to remove whatever gunk comes out, and then move on to your next nail.

## 2. Soak Them in Water

![Soak Them in Water](https://img.allw.mn/content/xj/pm/anfaiptm568e78639229c892315418_500x265.gif)

If you want to clean your entire nail, instead of beneath it, you can soak your hands in water. Just make sure that the water isn't cold, because warm water will do a better job of getting you all cleaned up. Don't forget to add soap to that water!

## 3. Use a Nail Brush to Scrub

![Use a Nail Brush to Scrub](https://img.allw.mn/content/rs/tf/k1uzl7q4568e78638b83e199345531_500x288.gif)

If you don't have a nail brush, then you should think about buying one. You can use it while you're soaking your hands in soapy water. In between dips, you can take your hand out and scrub at your nails, making sure to get the top and what's underneath it. It'll do a thorough job.

## 4. Soak Them in Apple Cider Vinegar

[https://media.giphy.com/media/QaDc2Wn7tfLFu/giphy.gif](https://media.giphy.com/media/QaDc2Wn7tfLFu/giphy.gif)
If you don't think that water is going to do the trick, you can always try soaking your nails in something else. Plenty of people swear by apple cider vinegar. Place some of it in a container, just deep enough so that you can fully submerge your nails, and leave them in there for five minutes. You can even use that nail brush if you'd like!

## 5. Moisturize Your Nails

![Moisturize Your Nails](https://img.allw.mn/content/pc/zk/q3pr51vs568e7864d1de7033824957_500x215.gif)

If you want to keep your nails in tiptop shape, you shouldn't forget to moisturize them. After all, your face isn't the only place that needs some moisture! It can never hurt to have some cuticle oil handy, as well.

## 6. Wear Gloves when Cleaning

![Wear Gloves when Cleaning](https://img.allw.mn/content/fo/sj/ml64z7b9568e7865a06d5144172135_500x280.gif)

It'll be easier for you to clean your nails if they aren't all that dirty to begin with. To avoid ruining them, you can try wearing gloves when you're cleaning. That way, you won't end up with dirt under your fingernails. Plus, you'll save your skin from being bogged down with chemicals. It's all around healthier to walk around in gloves.

## 7. Soak in Lemon Juice and Baking Soda

[https://media.giphy.com/media/jI3zz5KfaIeXu/giphy.gif](https://media.giphy.com/media/jI3zz5KfaIeXu/giphy.gif)
If you aren't happy with an apple cider vinegar soak, then there's one last thing you can try. If you have some lemon juice and baking soda, you can mix the two together. After you do that, you can dip your fingers into the mixture and let it work its magic.

Make sure you keep your nails clean, because you never know when your polish will chip and everything will be visible. What other tips do you have for ladies who want to thoroughly clean beneath their nails?
